## 3. Task 2: B2B SaaS Email Marketing Research
### Tools Installed
- **Supadata API**: Integrated for automated YouTube transcript extraction.
- **Python (Requests library)**: For automating API calls and data cleaning.

### Workflow
1. **Topic Selection:** Analyzed 8 trending topics; selected **"Newsletter / Email Marketing for B2B SaaS"** due to its high impact on LTV (Life Time Value) and Retention.
2. **Strategic Setup:** Configured Supadata API for high-speed data collection and established a structured research repository.
3. **Expert Selection Strategy:** Adopted a **"Multi-Angle Perspective"** approach. Instead of generic influencers, I targeted specific roles:

  - **Founders:** For high-level growth strategy.
   - **Specialists:** For technical infrastructure and deliverability.
    - **Copywriters:** For conversion psychology and messaging.
4. **Categorization Framework:** Organized content into 3 distinct pillars:
    - **Strategy/Framework:** Long-term systems and lifecycle mapping.
  - **Tactics/Execution:** Cold outreach, A/B testing, and specific "hacks".
   - **Case Study:** Real-world results and data-backed success stories.
5. **Curation & Refinement:** Conducted deep background checks on experts' LinkedIn history and YouTube content to ensure "high-signal" quality over volume.

### Issues & Solutions
- **Issue 1: Sourcing "High-Signal" Experts**
  - *Problem:* Marketing influencers are abundant, but genuine "practitioners" in the B2B SaaS niche often post sporadically or have their best insights scattered across multiple platforms.
  - *Solution:* Implemented a **Cross-Platform Verification** process. I cross-referenced LinkedIn engagement with YouTube technical depth to ensure the selected experts actually practice what they teach (e.g., verifying Taylor Haren's 50M+ email claim against his system walk-throughs).
- **Issue 2: Content Overlap and Classification**
  - *Problem:* Top experts often cover Strategy, Tactics, and Case Studies simultaneously, making it difficult to categorize their content without redundancy.
  - *Solution:* Applied a **"Primary Signal" Filter**. I mapped each expert's most "unique" strength to a specific project need—e.g., using Dan Martell exclusively for "Lifecycle Strategy" while using Taylor Haren for "Infrastructure Tactics"—ensuring the final playbook offers a diverse, non-repetitive knowledge base.
