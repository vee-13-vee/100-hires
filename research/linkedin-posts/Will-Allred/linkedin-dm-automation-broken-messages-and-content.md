# LinkedIn DM automation: text-style sequencing and no-ask content drops

**Expert:** Will Allred  
**Date Collected:** April 2026  
**Source:** LinkedIn (direct URL not captured)

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Treat automated LinkedIn DMs like a text thread: split what would be one email into several short messages with delays so it feels human, not like a blast.
- Lead with value, not a pitch: use automation chiefly to distribute helpful content (e.g., a report) rather than to hard-sell.
- A simple three-step pattern works: (1) reference the asset, (2) send the link, (3) light follow-up framed as convenience and curiosity ("would love to hear what you think") with no heavy ask.
- This approach can still drive real pipeline: stronger conversations, intros to decision-makers, and active opportunities—while being less abrasive for recipients.
- Context matters: author sells email intelligence and frames LI automation advice with that bias—recipients may still prefer fully manual outreach.
